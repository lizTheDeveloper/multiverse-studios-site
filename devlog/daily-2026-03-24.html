March 24, 2026 MVEE Precursors CotB

Plants Feed the World, and the Villagers Finally Move

Two days of across-the-board fixes. The food economy got a complete rethink. A steering bug that froze every agent in MVEE is gone. And Cultures of the Belt is playable again after a physics regression. Here's the rundown.

The plant economy

Glow pools are gone. They were a placeholder food source — magical glowing puddles that fed your population with no effort. They're removed entirely. Plants are now the sole food economy. Species need to cultivate, harvest, and distribute food from actual plant growth. This changes everything about early-game strategy — you can't just plop down near a glow pool and coast anymore.

Plant growth follows real-ish rules: sunlight, soil quality, water access. Different biomes support different crops. The food chain has actual links now instead of magic shortcuts.

When food is free, survival isn't interesting. When food requires work, every decision matters. The simulation woke up the moment we deleted the easy answer.

The steering bug

Every agent in MVEE was frozen. They'd pathfind correctly, pick a destination, and then... not move. The steering system had a normalization bug — direction vectors were being divided by their magnitude twice, collapsing them to near-zero. One line fix, but it took hours to find because the pathfinding logs showed correct intentions. The agents knew where to go. They just couldn't get their legs to work.

CotB playability restored

A physics regression in the orbital mechanics broke Cultures of the Belt's game loop. Asteroids weren't colliding correctly, which meant mining targets weren't generating debris fields, which meant the resource economy stalled. Traced it back to a coordinate system mismatch introduced during the belt physics cleanup. Fixed and verified — the belt is spinning again.

Three games, all moving forward. Play any of them free.
