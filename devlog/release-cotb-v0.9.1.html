March 28, 2026 CotB v0.9.1

Slower Mysteries, Better Ships

v0.9.0 was the biggest CotB update yet. v0.9.1 is the one that makes it breathe. We slowed down the mystery pacing, pushed early exposition later, and fixed the thing where clicking a ship didn't actually follow it.

Discovery pacing overhaul

The belt was revealing too much too fast. Anomalies, lore fragments, and narrative beats were piling up before players had time to settle into the rhythm of mining and building. Now the discovery curve is gentler — early days focus on survival and resource management. The strange stuff starts around day 10, when you've built enough context to care about what you're finding.

Early exposition has been deferred entirely. No more lore dumps in the first hour. The belt earns your curiosity before it rewards it.

A mystery that arrives too early is just noise. A mystery that arrives when you're already invested is a hook.

Ship click-to-follow

Click a ship, camera follows it. That's what it's supposed to do. In v0.9.0, the click target and the visual position would drift apart as orbits progressed — you'd click a ship and the camera would snap to where it was three seconds ago. Fixed. The follow camera now tracks the ship's current interpolated position, not its last-known physics tick.
