June 8, 2026 CotB v0.17.0

Open Airlock

We had 661 visitors a week and zero conversions. Every single one of them hit a mandatory sign-in wall before seeing a single asteroid. You had to create a Matrix account to find out whether you even liked the game. That wall is gone.

Guest Play Mode

Click "Play as Guest" and you're in the Belt. No account, no email, no friction. The full game loads immediately — mining, expeditions, factions, the whole simulation. Guests play the same game as authenticated players.

Sign-in prompts appear at natural value moments: when you try to save your colony, when you want to ask the ARIA AI advisor a question, and after fifteen minutes of play. Every prompt is dismissible. The game never stops you from playing — it just reminds you that your progress is temporary until you sign in.

What's Still Gated

Two things require an account: save slots and the ARIA advisor. Save slots need identity — we can't persist your colony without knowing who you are. ARIA uses LLM inference on every query, so we gate it to manage costs. Everything else is open.

Why This Matters

The pay-what-you-can button was unreachable. Players couldn't experience the game, so they couldn't decide it was worth supporting. Now visitors can mine asteroids, build alliances with Belt factions, and discover what makes the simulation tick — all before creating an account. The PWYC prompt reaches people who've actually played.

Cultures of the Belt is free to play. No account required. Pay what you can if you want to keep the lights on in the Belt.
