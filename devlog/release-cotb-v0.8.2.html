What ARIA Noticed

The Seven Civilizations left anomalies in the belt. You've been studying them — individually, sequentially, one at a time. That's how most players approach the research track. You pick a promising asteroid, deploy scouts, wait for ARIA to complete the analysis.

But the civilizations weren't separate stories. Their histories overlap in ways that only emerge when you look at more than one at a time. ARIA has been reading the anomalies in isolation. In v0.8.2, if you give her two to look at simultaneously, she starts drawing connections.

Anomaly Cross-Pollination

When you have scouts studying anomalies from two or more different civilization types at the same time, ARIA occasionally delivers a cross-pollination insight — a message that appears in the ARIA panel with a distinct synthesis accent, different from normal briefings. She's noticed something about how these two civilizations connect. How their failure modes mirror or invert each other. What that might mean for what's coming.

"The Seeker silence and the Dreamer resonance are inversions. One asked the final question and stopped speaking. The other felt the final emotion and couldn't stop. The question destroyed language. The emotion destroyed silence."

— ARIA, on studying Seeker and Dreamer anomalies simultaneously

There are 21 possible civilization pairs from the seven types. Each pair has one unique insight. If you're studying three types simultaneously, ARIA finds connections faster — 1.5% chance per second, versus 0.5% for two. The insights arrive as surprises. You don't know when one is coming, and you don't know which pair you'll unlock next.

The Synthesis Tab

A new Synthesis tab in the codex panel tracks your discoveries: N / 21 connections found, the civilization pair as a header, and the insight text listed newest first. If you haven't found any yet, it shows a hint about studying multiple types simultaneously. Discovered pairs persist through save/load — ARIA doesn't forget what she's figured out.

At 7, 14, and 21 discoveries, ARIA delivers synthesis-level revelations — connections that reframe what you thought you understood. Those revelations are why this tab matters. The 21/21 collection is long-term. The first synthesis message is not.
