March 28, 2026 CotB v0.9.0

The Belt Remembers

This is the biggest Cultures of the Belt update yet. Anomaly pacing, fleet identity, social bonds between crew, distress signals, and miners that actually manage their fuel. The belt is becoming a place with memory.

Anomaly pacing overhaul

Anomalies used to arrive on a flat random schedule. Now they build. Early belt life is quiet — routine mining, slow expansion. As your colony grows, anomalies scale with it. The belt notices you. Strange signals increase in frequency and complexity as your fleet footprint expands. It feels earned instead of random.

Fleet manifestos and mentorship bonds

Fleets now develop manifestos — emergent documents that reflect their shared experiences, priorities, and culture. A fleet that survived a collision event develops different values than one that grew through steady mining. Veteran crew members form mentorship bonds with new arrivals, passing down knowledge and shifting the fleet's collective personality over time.

When your miners remember what happened to them, and pass those memories to the next generation, you stop managing a fleet and start witnessing a culture.

Distress signals and smarter miners

Miners now broadcast distress signals when they're low on fuel or drifting off-course. Other ships in range can respond — haulers prioritize rescue, and nearby miners will adjust their own trajectories to assist if they have fuel to spare. The AI is no longer just path-planning; it's cooperating.

Mining AI itself got a significant upgrade. Delta-v budgeting now accounts for return trips before committing to an extraction. No more burning all your fuel reaching a rock and then drifting forever. Miners plan round trips.

Cultures of the Belt is free to play. Pay what you can if you like what you find in the rocks.
