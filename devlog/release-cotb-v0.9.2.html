March 29, 2026 CotB v0.9.2

Haulers to the Rescue

Miners were getting stranded. They'd burn through their delta-v chasing a rock, nail the extraction, and then run out of fuel on the way back. They'd drift into weird orbits around the base, slowly dying. That's fixed now.

Hauler rescue operations

Haulers now detect stranded miners and autonomously intercept them. When a miner's fuel drops below return-to-base threshold, the nearest hauler plots an intercept course, matches velocity, and transfers fuel. The hauler's larger fuel capacity means it can serve as a mobile depot — miners spin off, do their work, and the hauler handles the delta-v budget for the return trip.

This isn't a player-managed feature. It's emergent fleet behavior. You build haulers, assign them to a mining group, and the AI handles the rest. The physics are still non-negotiable — every Newton-meter of thrust costs fuel — but now your fleet is smart enough to not leave miners stranded.

Asteroid collisions

Big rocks hit little rocks now. The base can get struck by asteroids moving relative to it. Collisions are real — they have mass, momentum, and consequences. Your base might look stationary, but everything in the belt is moving, and now the simulation respects that.

The belt doesn't care about your plans. It throws rocks. Your fleet adapts or it doesn't. That's the game.

ARIA narration overhaul

ARIA — the station AI narrator — now speaks in show-don't-tell style. Instead of "A miner has low fuel," you'll hear something more like the miner's trajectory data shifting, the hauler's course correction. The belt tells its own story when you let the data speak.

Cultures of the Belt is free to play. Pay what you can if you like what you find in the rocks.
