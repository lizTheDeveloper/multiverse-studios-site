March 26, 2026 Precursors v0.4.45

The Ship Stirs

The ship feels more present now. It's not just a container for your Norns — it's a place that bears the marks of what's happened to it. Damage is visible. Repairs take effort. And the audio system no longer crashes when a species doesn't have songs yet.

Visible damage and weighted repairs

When the ship takes damage, you see it. Hull breaches leave scorch marks. Structural failures show cracks. Repairs aren't instant — they take time and resources, and the ship shows the process. A freshly repaired section looks different from one that's never been hit. The ship accumulates history.

This matters because Precursors is a game about evolution across generations. The ship is the one constant. Making it carry visible scars gives players a physical record of their journey — every collision, every hard landing, every close call written into the hull.

A ship that remembers what happened to it is a ship worth caring about. Pristine vessels don't tell stories.

Audio loading fix

Species in Precursors develop their own music through emergent cultural evolution. But not every species has songs yet — especially young ones. The audio system was trying to load song files for species that hadn't developed music, hitting a missing-file error and crashing the audio context entirely. Now it gracefully handles species without songs, falling back to ambient ship sounds until the culture develops its own.

Precursors is free to play. Watch evolution happen in real time.
