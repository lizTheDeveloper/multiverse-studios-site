The Belt Knows Your Name

Before v0.8.0, you were a nameless operation. Asteroids didn't care. Factions ignored you until you were powerful enough to notice. ARIA addressed you generically. The belt felt like a system you were running, not a place you were building something in.

That changes now.

Operation Identity

Name your mining operation at the start of a run. It's a small thing — a text field before you launch. But it threads through everything. Your operation name appears in the HUD, in ARIA's morning reports, in faction diplomacy messages, in trade dispatches. When the Vesta Compact sends a delegate, they address you by name. When your extraction quota hits a milestone, ARIA logs it against your operation's record.

Naming something changes how you relate to it. That's the whole point.

Faction Comms Log

The belt has been talking about you. You just couldn't hear it until now.

A new Comms tab in the ARIA panel surfaces ambient faction broadcasts and intercepted transmissions between rival operations. Each faction has a distinct voice — the Vesta Compact is terse and bureaucratic, the Ceres Free Miners are blunt, the deep-belt independents are cautious and clipped. Their transmissions react to what you're building. Hit a megastructure milestone and the chatter changes.

ARIA isn't just your advisor. She's been monitoring everyone else's signals too. The comms log is what she's been keeping to herself.

This is early. More faction voices, more reactive triggers, and deeper signal chains are coming. But the infrastructure is in — the belt is now a place where other people are doing things, and some of those things are about you.

Cultures of the Belt is free to play. Pay what you can if you like what you find in the rocks.
