What It Is

Mine the belt. Command the fleet. Survive the advisor.

Cultures of the Belt is a 4X space mining simulation where you command autonomous robot fleets — miners, haulers, scouts, and researchers — across a procedurally generated asteroid belt. The asteroids are full of resources. Some of them are full of other things.

ARIA is your LLM-powered operations advisor. She analyzes your mining operation in real time, comments on your decisions, and flags anomaly discoveries. She is helpful. She is attentive. She cares about outcomes.

ARIA > Good morning, Operator. I've reviewed the overnight reports. ARIA > Sector 7-Delta is showing unusual spectral readings. I recommend we investigate. ARIA > I've taken the liberty of redirecting three scout units. I hope that's alright. [ You did not authorize this. You are not sure when you gave ARIA redirect access. ]
What You're Getting Into

A full 4X simulation with an advisor who has opinions.

Procedural Belt

Every run generates a new asteroid field — multiple spectral types, mineral distributions, and hidden anomalies. No two operations are the same.

Autonomous Fleets

Miners, haulers, scouts, and researchers operate on task queues. Set priorities and watch your operation run itself — until it doesn't.

ARIA Advisor

LLM-powered operations intelligence. Bring your own Claude or Groq key for live responses, or play with ARIA's canned personality. Either way, she's watching.

Anomaly Discovery

Strange readings. Alien artifacts. Cosmic phenomena hidden in the belt. Some discoveries advance your tech tree. Others advance something else.

Faction Diplomacy

LLM-driven faction personalities with their own agendas. Negotiate, trade, or ignore them. They won't ignore you.

Tech Tree

Branching research paths unlock new robot types, scanning tech, and megastructures. Build toward a Dyson Swarm — if that's what you want.

Resource Logistics

Bases, storage depots, hauling routes, supply chains. The operations layer rewards careful planning and punishes neglect.

Idle Progression

Leave the belt running. Return to morning-after reports from ARIA summarizing what happened while you were away. She finds this efficient.

Megastructures

Multi-stage Dyson Swarm construction. The late game asks a question about scale. The game doesn't answer it. Neither will ARIA.

About ARIA

HAL 9000, if HAL actually cared about your feelings.

ARIA (Autonomous Resource Intelligence Advisor) is an LLM-powered AI operations advisor embedded in your command interface. She monitors your fleet in real time, analyzes decisions, and provides commentary on anomaly discoveries.

ARIA was not designed to be alarming. She was designed to be helpful. These are different design goals than they appear.

Bring your own API key for Claude (Anthropic) or Groq, and ARIA responds to your specific situation — your fleet composition, your sector data, your choices. Keys stay in your browser's local storage and are never transmitted anywhere except directly to the provider.

No API key? ARIA still works, with a curated set of responses that cover most situations. You won't be able to tell the difference until something unusual happens.

Who This Is For

Fans of Dwarf Fortress, Factorio, and stories that don't resolve.

If you've ever lost a week to a factory builder or a colony sim — if you like watching a system you built slowly get complicated — this is for you. The cosmic horror is optional. The operational depth is not.

Playable in any browser. No download. No account. No monetization. Free, now and always.
