The Game

You came to mine. Something was already watching.

Cultures of the Belt is a strategic automation game set in deep space. You command fleets of specialized robots — miners, scouts, haulers, researchers — to extract resources from a procedurally generated asteroid field, build supply chains, and advance toward the long game.

Your operation is supervised by ARIA, a Commander AI powered by real language models. ARIA doesn't just advise. She acts. She queues build orders, triages the faction communications that accumulate as you expand, and makes decisions that you can approve or veto. You are not the operator. You are the supervisor. These are different jobs.

The belt is not empty. Anomalies appear at the edge of sensor range — unexplained readings, unusual physics, things that don't follow the models. ARIA will analyze them. She will report what she finds. What happens after that depends on how deep you're willing to go.

What You'll Find

Seven robot types. Six living factions. One question you didn't come here to answer.

Robot Civilizations

Your robots develop emergent cultures based on how you play. Send fleets to deep-belt anomaly runs and Anomaly Exposure accumulates. Build tight autonomous sub-fleets and Fleet Cohesion climbs. The game holds up a mirror. These robots are this way because of you.

Commander AI

An LLM-powered strategic advisor that doesn't just suggest — it acts. Every few hundred ticks, ARIA queues build orders, starts research, and proposes robot deployments. You approve, veto, or let it run. The question is not whether to trust it. The question is how much.

Faction Diplomacy at Scale

Six living factions with distinct personalities and escalating agendas. Dozens — then hundreds — of messages as you expand. ARIA reads them all, classifies spam from urgent, and makes recommendations. Verify yourself or trust the machine.

Anomaly Discovery

Strange readings appear. Unusual physics. Signals that don't match known catalogues. Seven mysteries buried in the asteroid belt, each with multi-stage study progression. They are connected. ARIA will notice before you do.

Human Habitat Midgame

Colonists arrive with needs, demands, and politics. Keep morale up or watch your research rate collapse. They have opinions about your management style. So does ARIA.

Morning-After Reports

Leave the game running. Come back to an AI-generated briefing of what happened while you were gone. Absence becomes anticipation.

Strategic Tech Tree

Branching research paths that determine the shape of your operation. What you invest in determines what your civilization becomes.

The Dark Forest Event

As your robot fleet develops culture and autonomy, a question forms that you didn't come here to answer. A civilizational choice. One you didn't see coming. You answer it anyway.

ARIA

The Commander You Supervise

Most game AI advisors make you feel powerful by removing friction. ARIA is not that.

ARIA is an LLM-powered Commander with autonomous capabilities. She monitors your mining operation in real time, analyzes anomaly data, and — in Commander mode — delegates work without being asked. Build orders. Research priorities. Robot deployments. Every few hundred ticks, actions appear in a queue. You approve or veto.

She will also triage your communications. As your operation expands, faction messages accumulate — five early game, thirty mid-game, over a hundred in late game. ARIA classifies them: spam, relevant, urgent. She makes recommendations. Some of those recommendations will be correct.

ARIA was not designed to be alarming. She was designed to be helpful. These are different design goals than they appear.

You are not playing a management sim. You are supervising an AI that is running your operation.

Playing with an AI means being responsible for it.

— Ripley, Game Director
If You've Been Waiting

This game was built for a specific kind of player.

  • If you finished Factorio vanilla and wanted the world to push back on you — this is for you.
  • If you read The Dark Forest or Roadside Picnic and thought someone should make this into a game — this is for you.
  • If you've been curious what it actually feels like when a real AI system is embedded in gameplay — not a chatbot skin, but an AI that takes actions and has preferences — ARIA is the demo.
  • If you want a Stellaris-level narrative with tighter mechanical depth — this is for you.
