Never Ever Land

What if Wendy said no?

A feminist retelling of Peter Pan — interactive fiction about the stories we get trapped inside.

Read & Play — Free About the Game
The Premise

A story about who gets to leave the story.

Wendy Darling arrives in Neverland and immediately understands the deal: she has been brought here to become useful. To mother. To mend. To wait. The island expects it. Peter assumes it. The Lost Boys need it.

She says no.

The island — which is not accustomed to being refused — begins to come apart. Not violently. Structurally. The narrative mechanics that held Neverland together start to fracture, and Wendy finds herself navigating a world where the story itself has become unreliable.

"I will not become furniture in a house you build out of me." Wendy Darling, Chapter One
Three Layers of Story

Read. Choose. Generate.

📖

The Reading Layer

Seven chapters of beautifully typeset prose. Day and night themes. A complete story you can simply read, like a book that happens to live in your browser.

⚔

The Branching Layer

An Ink-powered interactive narrative woven through the story. At key moments, you choose. The narrative forks. Wendy's path through Neverland becomes yours.

✨

The Generative Layer

When the written story ends, Claude continues it. AI-generated scenes extend the narrative infinitely, cached for all readers. You explore the story space together.

What It's About

Care labor. Analytical patience. The trap of being needed.

Peter Pan is public domain, but the questions it asks are not settled. Who maintains the world while the adventurers adventure? What happens when the person doing the emotional labor notices the pattern? What does a children's story look like when the children are allowed to grow up?

Never Ever Land is not an adaptation. It is an argument with the original — conducted with love, conducted in its own language, conducted by someone who read the book carefully enough to notice what it was asking of its girl characters.

"Some people speak of intuition as though it were a delicate bell. It is not. It is a trapdoor with excellent timing." Chapter One
Under the Hood

Built with love and open tools.

✍

Ink Narrative Engine

Branching narrative powered by Inkle's open-source Ink scripting language, compiled to JSON and run in the browser with inkjs.

🧠

Claude-Powered Generation

When pre-written content ends, Claude Opus generates continuation scenes that maintain voice, theme, and narrative coherence.

🌐

Shared Story Tree

Generated scenes are cached in SQLite. Every reader who takes the same path finds the same continuation. The story grows collectively.

Read it. Play it. Pay what you want.
