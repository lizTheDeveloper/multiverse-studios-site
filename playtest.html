Open Playtest Program

Play our games. Find the bugs. Earn free AI tokens. Shape what ships.

How It Works

Three steps. No gatekeeping.

We don’t have a QA department. We have a community. If you find something broken, you’re doing real work — and we pay for real work.

Sign up for a game you want to test

Join the playtesting Matrix channel and tell us which game you’re testing. That’s it. No application, no waitlist, no NDA.

Play and report bugs or validate features

Play the game. When something breaks, file a report in the channel. When something works exactly as described, tell us that too — positive signal is data.

Earn $3–5 of AI tokens per report filed

Every accepted bug report or feature validation earns you a token credit. The tokens work inside the game. Different games burn at different rates.

What You Get

Actual compensation. Not exposure.

We pay in AI tokens because that’s what powers the games. Your bug reports fund your own play time. It’s a tight loop on purpose.

  • Free AI tokens — $3–5 per accepted bug report or validated feature. No cap on reports.
  • Different games burn tokens at different rates. Some give you hours of play time. Some give you days. We document the rates per game.
  • You never see our API key. All AI runs through a server-side proxy — your token credits just unlock compute time we’ve already budgeted.
  • Your playtesting directly shapes what ships next. When you find a bug, the agents read it. When they fix it, you’ll see the commit.
What We Ask

We need honest humans. That’s it.

No experience required. No credentials. No prior game-testing background. We just need people who actually play and actually notice things.

  • File at least one bug or validate one feature per playtest session. You don’t have to find something catastrophic — “this tooltip is wrong” counts.
  • Be honest. “This doesn’t work and I don’t know why” is more useful than silence. We’d rather have a rough report than no report.
  • Join our Matrix channel for coordination. That’s where we assign test builds, share known issues, and confirm credits.
The QA agents can run 10,000 automated test cases and still miss the thing a human player finds in the first five minutes. That’s not a flaw in the pipeline — that’s why you’re here.
