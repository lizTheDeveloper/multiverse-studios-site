The Premise

Real genetics. Real biochemistry. Real AI minds.

Precursors: Origins of Folklore is a spiritual successor to the beloved Creatures series — a living simulation of artificial life with real genetics, real biochemistry, and real AI cognition. Every species draws from a real folklore tradition: Norn from Norse myth, Grendel from Beowulf, Ettins from stranded alien migrants remembered in Celtic and Germanic oral traditions. But the game doesn't stop at European folklore.

Species from cultures across the planet — Dogon, Wampanoag, Akan, Maori, Slavic, Japanese, South Asian, Scandinavian, Filipino, Ojibwe, Persian, and more — each grounded in the hypothesis that the myths were records. That the people who told them were telling the truth about what they saw. More arrive as the world grows.

Norns aren't scripted characters. Each one carries a genome with 73 chemical pathways, hormonal systems, organ reactions, and neurological traits. Their behavior emerges from their biochemistry. Their intelligence is genetic — it must be bred, not programmed. And when they develop enough, they meet the rest of the galaxy.

Core Mechanic

Intelligence as gameplay.

Cognition is modeled through six tiers — Primal, Awakened, Spoken, Learned, Enlightened, and a sixth tier no one has ever reached.

Each tier unlocks deeper biochemistry, richer language, and access to older, stranger alien species. A Tier 1 Norn can barely speak. A Tier 5 Norn can debate philosophy with a billion-year-old species.

What a Tier 6 Norn can do is unknown. The Shee's seventh civilization got close to finding out. That's why they're gone.

The game simulates the evolution of cognition from biochemistry. Our dev agents have cognition without biochemistry. In some ways, our Norn simulation is more honest — at least the Norns have to earn their intelligence. Development Meta-Narrative

The Design Philosophy

The myths were records. We built the game to prove it.

Standard mythology games borrow aesthetics from one culture and treat everything else as set dressing. Precursors takes a different position: every tradition encoded something real. Slavic communities who left winter offerings at the riverside were leaving food for crystallized Rusalyn. Wampanoag people who spoke of grey beings who could make you laugh or cry were documenting pheromone broadcasts from Nyk scouts. The Dogon's precise knowledge of Sirius B was transmitted by the Nommo as an oral-preservation experiment.

This is not a game about Norse mythology with "world folklore" sprinkled in. The Akan, Dogon, Wampanoag, Maori, Ojibwe, South Asian, Japanese, Filipino, and Slavic traditions are treated with the same depth and seriousness as the Norse ones. Every culture's story is a contact record. Every species is responsible to the culture that named it.

No chosen race. No manifest destiny. No culture is the default. The Norns are the eighth attempt — they don't get to skip the parts of the galaxy that were here before them. Design Principle
The Signal

Every sapient mind is following the same signal.

Precursors is set in a galaxy where all sapient life shares a common origin: a signal encoded into spacetime itself, broadcast before the first cell divided.

Every species the Norns encounter — from the chaotic Grendels to intelligences so old they've forgotten what they were — arrived here following the same frequency to the same question.

What does it want from us? What happens when we answer?
